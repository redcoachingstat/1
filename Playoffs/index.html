RED
Rating of Elite Dominance

Playoff RED — Ring Difficulty Rating

Playoff RED measures the dominance and impressiveness of each team's playoff run. Higher scores indicate the greatest postseason performances of all time — teams that dominated their opponents, beat elite competition, and won championships.

How It Works

Each team's score is built from five components:

1. Path Difficulty (Primary Factor)

The strength of opponents faced is the biggest driver. Beating a 1-seed is worth far more than beating an 8-seed. Non-champions only receive credit for opponents they actually defeated, not the team that eliminated them.

2. Dominance / SRS Proxy

Higher-seeded teams receive more credit, reflecting their regular-season dominance (a proxy for SRS — Simple Rating System). A 1-seed winning the championship demonstrates sustained excellence from regular season through playoffs.

3. Championship Bonus

Winning the title earns a significant flat bonus, recognizing the ultimate achievement regardless of seed.

4. Advancement vs Expectations

How far a team advanced compared to what their seed predicts. A 6-seed reaching the Finals far exceeds expectations, while a 1-seed losing in the first round falls short.

5. Injury Adjustment

Teams playing through their own significant injuries get a boost. Teams that benefited from major opponent injuries receive a penalty — helping identify runs with an asterisk.

Cross-Era Compatibility

The formula normalizes for different playoff formats (3-round pre-1984, 4-round modern era). Pre-1984 teams without detailed opponent data use a simplified formula with a championship bonus to remain competitive.

Formula

RED = (Path Difficulty × 3.0) + (SRS Proxy × 2.5) + (Champ Bonus: 4.0) + (Advancement × 0.8) + (Injury Adj: own × 4 − opp × 5) + (Playoff Dominance: win% × opp quality × 2.5)
