How RED Works

RED (Rating of Elite Dominance) is a team season performance metric. It measures how dominant a team truly is — combining their record relative to league-average with how efficiently they convert scoring margin into wins.

The Core Idea

Every team has a "true" performance level hidden in their scoring margins. RED captures the gap between actual wins and what point differential predicts. This makes RED era-neutral — so you can compare the 2025–26 Spurs to the 1975–76 Warriors on the same scale.

The Formula

(A) Quality = Actual Wins − (GP × 0.5) → Wins above league-average pace (B) Efficiency = Actual Wins − Pythagorean Expected Wins Pythagorean Win% = PtsFor¹³·⁹¹ ÷ (PtsFor¹³·⁹¹ + PtsAgainst¹³·⁹¹) → Winning more than scoring margin predicts? RED = (0.70 × Quality) + (0.30 × Efficiency)

Interpretation

Positive RED: Winning above pace and/or converting margin efficiently. Truly dominant teams score highest.

Negative RED: Below .500 pace and/or underperforming point differential.

The efficiency signal (30%): Rewards teams squeezing extra wins beyond what raw scoring predicts — defense-first teams, clutch units.
